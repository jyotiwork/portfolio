Depco is a developer productivity and collaboration platform which is used in the software development life cycle by developers, architects and project managers. Designit is working with Wipro to reimagine Depco.

For my graduation project, the brief was to reimagine the task management and knowledge base experience for Depco, to improve developer productivity and enable collaboration across teams.
I collaborated with the research team to understand the domain, the problem faced by the users, and the reasoning behind their recommendations. Thereafter, I designed the knowledge base and task management experience for Depco which helps developers, architects, and PMs find the relevant resources from documents to experts. Project Brief

To reimagine the task management and knowledge base experience for Depco, in order to improve developer productivity and enable collaboration across teams. 

A knowledge base is an online library of information about a product or topic. It can be used to form toolkits and find experts.

Task management is the process of managing a task through its life cycle. It involves planning, testing, tracking, and reporting.



The Solution

I designed a knowledge base that not only enables users to find documents and other forms of knowledge resources but also helps them connect with experts in the field within the organization. A simple task management experience was designed to help them plan and track their projects and tasks.

The knowledge base and task management experience of Depco were designed from scratch based on the challenges identified during the research phase as a part of my project brief.



Design Process

The design process was divided into two phases-
Phase 1: Discover
The discovery phase was a team effort that included 2 researchers, the project manager and me, UX/UI design intern. It started from stakeholder alignment, followed by contextual study, primary research, and then synthesis and analysis of the research.

Phase 2: Design
The design phase was an individual effort. Keeping the design guiding principles in mind from the research outcomes, I divided the design realisation activities into four parts - starting from research, followed by wireframing, visualizing, and finally prototyping my designs.

COVID - 19 Pandemic took an unprecedented test on education. It was tough but technology and products are here to share the load, so is Spot. This product is valid and needed irrespective of a pandemic or not, it will make students’ lives easier, college is hard enough already.



Testing

Tested with 8 people out of which 75% (6 people) were satisfied.
Suggestions from users:
▶ If the user is adding a product in the cart, it should show whether it is in the kitchen or not/ last added.
▶ If the product user is adding in the cart is unhealthy, give warning.
▶ Features like food journal with personal comments, community of people pursuing healthy lifestyle could be added.
▶ Micro-animation and illustration could be added.
There is a lot of scope for reiteration.



Key Learnings

I learned a lot more about people's behaviour and their reasons through this project. Also, learnt how important it is to reiterate, keep users at the heart of everything we do and use the double diamond framework better.
