GRADUATION PROJECT • UI/UX DESIGN • PROTOTYPING


Depco is a developer productivity and collaboration platform which is used in the software development life cycle by developers, architects and project managers. Designit is working with Wipro to reimagine Depco.

For my graduation project, the brief was to reimagine the task management and knowledge base experience for Depco, to improve developer productivity and enable collaboration across teams.
I collaborated with the research team to understand the domain, the problem faced by the users, and the reasoning behind their recommendations. Thereafter, I designed the knowledge base and task management experience for Depco which helps developers, architects, and PMs find the relevant resources from documents to experts. It also enabled them to plan and track their projects and tasks efficiently.

Project Brief

To reimagine the task management and knowledge base experience for Depco, in order to improve developer productivity and enable collaboration across teams. 

A knowledge base is an online library of information about a product or topic. It can be used to form toolkits and find experts.
(A toolkit is a collection of documents that a project manager uses to introduce and inform developers about a project. It may include FAQs, guidebooks, blogs, articles, video demonstrations, glossaries, courses etc.)

Task management is the process of managing a task through its life cycle. It involves planning, testing, tracking, and reporting.



The Solution

I designed a knowledge base that not only enables users to find documents and other forms of knowledge resources but also helps them connect with experts in the field within the organization. A simple task management experience was designed to help them plan and track their projects and tasks.

The knowledge base and task management experience of Depco were designed from scratch based on the challenges identified during the research phase as a part of my project brief.



Design Process

The design process was divided into two phases-
Phase 1: Discover
The discovery phase was a team effort that included 2 researchers, the project manager and me, UX/UI design intern. It started from stakeholder alignment, followed by contextual study, primary research, and then synthesis and analysis of the research.

Phase 2: Design
The design phase was an individual effort. Keeping the design guiding principles in mind from the research outcomes, I divided the design realisation activities into four parts - starting from research, followed by wireframing, visualizing, and finally prototyping my designs.

Visual Design

I tried two directions. First was modern and stylised where I followed the current design trends. Second direction was minimal and simple. I went ahead with the minimal direction because it aligned with the user needs, enterprise design and I chose functionality over form.





Searching the
Knowledge Base

The user can search for projects, people (experts and original publishers of the knowledge resources), guides, blogs, code files, videos, audios, communities and trainings in the knowledge base. They can further select multiple files together to form their own repositories and share it with their team.

User Cases:
▶ Project Manager finds colleagues with similar project experiences from the knowledge base and recruits the right people for their new project.
▶ PM searches for relevant files to make a toolkit to onboard the developers onto a new project.
▶ Architect searches for reference material of past use cases, ready to use templates, code files, guides, videos to take technical decisions and kickstart the project.
▶ Developer finds reference material or experts for guidance if he gets stuck somewhere in the ongoing project.




Project Planning

Once a project is assigned to the project manager, they plan, execute, monitor and document it. The PM starts planning the project by creating a project roadmap which consists of milestones and their respective timelines. The milestones are are further divided into tasks which are assigned to the team.





Adding to the
Knowledge Base

In order to give back to the community, the users can also make their contribution by adding files and other knowledge resources to the knowledge base. It can be done throughout the project life cycle by all the user personas. The format of adding files to the knowledge base is uniform and strict so that the person consuming those resources is at ease.




Creating Reports

Documentation of the project progress may be done by the project manager throughout the project in the form of reports which can be shared with the leadership and clients. The platform helps PMs by generating reports smart enough to sound like a project manager. It also includes all the necessary details and statistics in the report.
