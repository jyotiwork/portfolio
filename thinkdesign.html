NIFT Website Audit

INTERNSHIP • DESIGN RESEARCH


National Institute of Fashion Technology Content Management System (NIFT CMS) is an internal portal website for students and faculties. In this project, only students’ side is being analyzed.

This project was done as a part of my design research internship at Think Design.



Goal

To create an audit report and make recommendations for NIFT CMS website and understand the user experience of its users (students).



Objective

▶ To study Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) and Heuristic Principles.
▶ To observe and interview students to understand their experience while using the NIFT CMS website.

Accessibility

I studied the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), types of disabilities, everything related and made myself an accessibility checklist for myself.



Accessibility Checklist

✔ Enough color contrast ( ≥ 4.5:1 )
✔ Color not alone to make critical information understandable
✔ Usable focus states
✔ Labels or instructions with form fields and inputs
✔ Useful alternative text for images and other non text content
✔ Correct markup for content
✔ Keyboard navigation

Key Observations

After conducting the survey with about 40 students, the following are some of the key observations:
▶ 48% students check their account 3-4 times a week, spending 5-15 mins.
▶ Main purpose to log in was attendance and marks whereas occasional clicks were birthdays, student profile and subjects.
▶ Students described their experience as confusing, not intuitive or user friendly.



Key Insights

▶ Students get confused due to repeated names of the headings which lead to few additional steps before they can get the information they were seeking.
▶ Some links and buttons are misleading which makes the user frustrated.
▶ Responsive mobile version of the website is more difficult to use than the laptop version; the icons and links are small and difficult to click on.
▶ Presence of irrelevant information causes frustration.

Heuristic Analysis

Analysis was done for all 10 heuristics.



✔ Visibility of system status

The system should always keep users informed about what is going on, through appropriate feedback within reasonable time.
When the window is open for a while and no activity is detected, it logs off and when the user clicks anywhere - “Session expired, please login” message is shown keeping the user informed.

Heuristic Violated: No




✔ Consistency and Standards

Users should not have to wonder whether different words, situations, or actions mean the same thing. Follow platform conventions.
Lack of consistency and standards ends up confusing the user often. Similar words lead to different pages altogether and different words lead to similar pages.

Heuristic Violated: Yes
Recommendation: Proper sorting of information and putting it under valid labels and ensuring no two links overlap will solve this problem.
